WebGL Garbage

A demo of webgl. Copied from webgl2fundamentals. WebGL takes the web to the next level. One can now use their graphics card through the web browser. This allows web applications to leverage decent computational power. I'm personally excited by the fact that I can include some javascript on my static webpage that could for example, simulate Maxwells Equations (the equations that describe how light behaves under the wave model of light). This means that for example, I could write my simulation in javascript and then present and share my simulation realtime during a meeting.

I'm going to be practicing WebGL, which means I need to actually do WebGL. Thus I've made a separate webpage WebGL Garbage, which has my exercies thus far.

Quaternions

IDK I just had to talk about quaternions. Quaternions are stupid cool. They "cover" the sphere twice. Quaternions are the supreme way to encode rotation, as they defeat the gimble lock problem. Finally, quaternions are a natural extension of the imaginary number. A subset of quaternions, the one's with unit magnitude, actually have a mapping to SU(2). This means that quaternions not only encode rotations, but they also encode spin 1/2 particle shit on the Blochsphere. Quaternions are a mathematical tool equivalent of a double barrel shotgun and flame thrower combo, wielded by a hot bisexual. That is to say, Quaternions are fucking cool. They are getting their own page. I'm obsessed with them. Here is my page on quaternions.
